##  Overview
This work examines how cost efficiency evolves across major Indian banks over time. Instead of looking at performance in isolation, the study focuses on whether banks are **moving toward a common efficiency level (convergence)**.

The analysis covers the period **2015–2023** and includes leading public, private, and foreign banks operating in India.

---

##  Key Contribution
The study introduces a **structured analytical approach to understand efficiency convergence**, with a focus on:

- Tracking how banks adjust their cost structures over time  
- Comparing convergence patterns across different types of banks  
- Working effectively even when the dataset is relatively small  

This makes the approach relevant not only for academic research but also for **real-world financial and analytics applications** where data limitations are common.

---

##  Approach (High-Level)
The analysis is based on:

- Panel data analysis of banking indicators  
- Use of financial performance variables such as:
  - Cost-to-income ratio  
  - Profitability metrics  
  - Risk indicators  
- Incorporation of broader economic conditions  

The focus is on identifying patterns, relationships, and adjustment dynamics rather than just static comparisons.

---

##  Key Insights
- There is **evidence of convergence in cost efficiency** among Indian banks  
- **Foreign banks** show the strongest movement toward efficiency benchmarks  
- **Public sector banks** follow with moderate convergence  
- **Domestic private banks** show relatively weaker convergence patterns  

Additional insights highlight the relationship between:
- Profitability and efficiency  
- Risk factors and cost structures  
- Operational performance and long-term sustainability
